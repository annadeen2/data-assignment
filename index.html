Express Bus Scheduling in the Bronx

Last year, the MTA announced that it would make changes to express bus service between the Bronx and Manhattan. These changes included cutting midday and evening routes, and rerouting two bus lines away from Central Park at Mount Sinai and Museum Mile. Due to overwhelming pushback from residents, the MTA delayed changes, which would have been implemented in fall 2020, for one year.

Despite the delays in MTA changes, poor service still impacts Bronx residents. Read below to find out more.

Accessibility: Buses are often the only accessible transportation option. Many neighborhoods do not have nearby train lines, and those that do may not have accessible train stations. If they do, the stations may not have working elevators.

Transportation: Many residents, including the elderly, rely on express buses to go downtown for jobs, classes, and shopping, especially since local businesses have experienced a high rate of closures due to COVID-19 and increasingly high rents.

Bunching: As it is, passengers experience buses “bunching.” In other words, someone might wait at the bus stop for 45 minutes, before three buses arrive at once.

Overcrowding: Passengers may also experience overcrowding on the express bus lines. For example, the BX3 bus (an express bus) is the most crowded bus in all five buroughs. This line serves Bronx Community College, which has an enrollment of 10,000+, and runs through areas hit the hardest by COVID-19. The BX3 bus line runs small buses, as opposed to the longer, accordion-style buses.

Budget: Despite City Council Members like Fernanda Cabrera (District 14) having requested more frequent service and larger buses, the MTA says it may have to reduce subway and bus service by 40% if the agency does not receive $12 billion in federal aid.

By Anna Deen, Oct. 9, 2020
